A Brownstone with two moods

In some of our wildest dreams, everything is located in a Brooklyn brownstone. Restaurants. Grocery stores. Exterminators. For the most part, this is impossible — except at Prima.

Prima occupies the bottom floor of a brownstone on Greene Avenue, just east of Fort Greene Park. By morning the front window fills with people leaning over cappuccinos and laminated pastries, soft jazz on the speakers, a single shaft of sun across the marble counter.

By evening, the same room becomes an intimate wine bar — candlelight, glasses pulled by hand, a rotating list of bottles cultivated by the team. There's almost always at least one second date in the back, and in summer the backyard fills with strings of lights and shared bottles.

Two lives. One brownstone. The same warm room, lit two different ways.

After the espresso machine clicks off

An intimate wine bar, on the same marble.

The room reverses itself. The pastry case empties, candles come out, and the back tables fill up with people who came here on purpose. The list rotates often — ask whoever pours you, they'll know what's open.

"A coffee shop and wine bar in a Clinton Hill brownstone. They host frequent events, and they have a backyard where you can lounge with a bottle in the summer." — The Infatuation

Reservations aren't required. Walk in, sit down, ask what's good.
